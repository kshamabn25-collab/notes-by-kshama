The Meeting That Looked Fine on Paper

A quiet lesson from a moment I didn’t expect to matter.

It was a Monday morning, the calendar full of back-to-back meetings. My coffee had gone cold, and the project sync was supposed to be routine.

Halfway through, I noticed tension in the room. Everyone nodded politely, but the questions were safe, the smiles faint. The plan looked perfect on slides, yet something felt off.

I stopped presenting and started listening. Observing instead of speaking, noting subtleties in tone and timing. That day, the PMO’s real work became visible: building trust, not enforcing processes.

Over time, I realized the value of these quiet observations. A report alone never changes outcomes — relationships and subtle influence do.

Some lessons don’t arrive as feedback — they arrive as silence.
