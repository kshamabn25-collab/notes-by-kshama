Learning to Pause in a World That Rewards Speed

Leadership is often found in small, quiet moments.

I used to rush through feedback sessions, trying to cover all points in 10 minutes. One day, a student asked a question that stopped me in my tracks.

Instead of answering quickly, I paused. Let the silence sit. Let them think. The response that came was far better than any prepared line I could have given.

That week became a lesson in restraint. Teaching isn’t about speed; it’s about creating space for learning to happen.

Every day since, I’ve remembered: pausing allows reflection, depth, and true understanding.

Pausing didn’t slow me down — it let learning happen.
