Lessons Learned

What improved schedule accuracy, what improved schedule truth, and what I’d repeat.

Lessons Learned

What worked

  • Integrated scheduling surfaced “portfolio truth” earlier than individual trackers
  • Near-critical monitoring reduced surprise slippage even when dates looked stable
  • Dependency ownership + next-step discipline improved closure rate
  • Sensitivity scenarios made tradeoffs explicit (and decisions faster)

What I’d improve next time

  • Lock evidence standards earlier (what counts as “confirmed”)
  • Reduce churn by tightening change-control thresholds
  • Strengthen readiness definitions for commissioning and handover
A schedule can be correct in isolation and still be wrong in context. The IMS is how you protect committed delivery dates from hidden drift.
